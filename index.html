Module Core Concepts

Understanding these fundamental concepts is the first step on your journey into innovative product development.

Project

A project is a temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result.

Product

A quantifiable artefact. In computing, it usually refers to software, systems or services. In other contexts, it can refer to any physical or intangible good or service that is provided or sold.

Value

The value, importance, or usefulness of something. Value is **subjective** and depends on different stakeholders such as customers, organisations, and society.

Value Delivery System

A series of strategic activities within an organization designed to create value, of which projects are a key component.

Predictive Methods

Traditional development methods, such as the waterfall model, require most of the planning to be completed at the beginning of the project.

Agile Methods

Key Point. Welcome change and respond to uncertainty through short-cycle iterations and continuous feedback.
